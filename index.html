Hi! I am Hengxu. I am an MS Financial Economics
student at Columbia Business School. I graduated from Sun Yat-sen University, with BS. in Mathematics and Applied Mathematics and BA. in Accounting.

I am a quantitative researcher applying intersection of mathematical modeling, optimization and econometrics to areas of societal impact and unintended consequence of machine learning (e.g., fairness), digital technologies, social networks, financial strategies and decisions.

Currently, I am fortunate to work with Professor Rajeev Kohli, Professor Shunyuan Zhang and Professor Kamel Jedidi. I greatly appreciate them for the support and advice. Previously, I worked in Machine Learning Group, Microsoft Research. You can find our previous research here.

My recent research interests include:
(1) Fair & Causal Machine Learning: how to design robust algorithms and systems to attack inequity in management strategies and social activities? How to interpret the distribution drifting of human preferences/company decisions?
(2) Dynamic Learning: how to model the real-world feedback of a decision (choice, pricing, recommendation, competing) and how to coordinate multiple correlated sequential actions?
(3) Economics Value of Unstructured Data: how to recognize and utilize patterns in heterogeneous data (networks, digital market, healthcare, finance), in order to interpret or forecast for long-term and short term demand/supply objectives?
(4) Diffusion and Peer Effects of Creativity: how does business creativity diffuse in a network? How originality impacts peers and what are the societal outcomes?
