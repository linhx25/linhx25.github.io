Hi! I am Hengxu. I am an MS Financial Economics student at Columbia Business School. I graduated from Sun Yat-sen (Zhongshan) University, with degrees of BS. in Mathematics and Applied Mathematics and BA. in Accounting.

I am a quantitative researcher focusing on several directions in applying Machine Learning into computational research. My goal is to discover knowledge and recognize patterns behind data to the end of business needs.

Currently, I work with Professor Rajeev Kohli, Professor Shunyuan Zhang and Professor Kamel Jedidi. I thank them for the support and advice. Previously, I worked in Machine Learning Group, Microsoft Research. I am fortunate to be co-advised by Dr. Weiqing Liu and Dong Zhou. You can find our previous research here.

My research interests include:
(1) Fairness & Causal Machine Learning: how to deal with the distribution drifting of human preferences/company decisions? How to design robust algorithms and systems to attack inequity in management strategies and social activities?
(2) Reinforcement Learning: how to model the real-world feedback of a decision and how to coordinate multiple correlated sequential decisions?
(3) Data Mining: how to recognize and utilize patterns in heterogeneous data, in order to interpret or forecast for demand/supply objectives?
