Past Research Work

Evaluating Fairness in Sequential Decision Making
Jerry Anunrojwong, Hengxu Lin, Tianyu Wang, Wenxin Zhang.

Despite contextual bandits' theoretical and empirical success in achieving optimal regret, decision-makers are increasingly concerned about fairness considerations to prevent disparate treatment across time and individuals. In this paper, we extensively evaluate different fairness metrics of contextual bandit algorithms. We leverage on the real-world classification and pricing datasets where fairness is a concern to empirically investigate how these fairness metrics and associated proposed algorithms can bring different practical implications across time. Furthermore, we propose an algorithm that enjoys better fairness-regret frontier compared with existing fairness-aware and unfair algorithms in the empirical performance.

Do Unique Product Images Speak to Customers? Evidence from Airbnb
Hengxu Lin, working with Shunyuan Zhang.

The research studies the uniqueness of Airbnb images and its relationship to the consumer response reflected in property demand. I focus on two questions: 1) how does an image unveil the uniqueness of the Airbnb property? 2) How is the uniqueness related to consumer response? I propose a generalizable machine learning approach to learn uniqueness from the product images, which is further linked to product distinctiveness. I implement the methodology and analysis of Airbnb listing images. The findings may suggest how the hosts can make their Airbnb listing stand out by taking more unique photographs. This research fills the literature gap on uniqueness between behavior (need for uniqueness) and quantitative (measurement of uniqueness) sides.

Dynamic Fair Lending in Online Auto Loan Market
Master Thesis, 2023

The paper addresses lending discrimination against minority consumers in the online auto-loan market, where data and algorithmic biases can lead to unfair lending decisions. I propose a novel fair contextual bandit model that incorporates static group unfairness and temporal individual unfairness as constraints in the learning objective. The model achieves comparable prediction accuracy to other benchmarks while significantly improving multiple fairness metrics. The optimal policy balances temporal and group unfairness by adjusting the tradeoff and lookback horizon in an adaptive way. Specifically, the model accepts more minority and low-income borrowers and values borrowers with higher education years and older age. Overall, the proposed method provides a promising technical solution to mitigate consumer lending discrimination.

Black-Litterman Assest Allocation with Generative Adversarial Networks
Undergrad Thesis, 2020

The prior returns' distribution and the views of the Black-Litterman assets allocation model has been researched for years. With the development of deep learning, the generative adversarial network (GAN) was used in stock prediction recently. The research originally builds an asset allocation framework based on the Black-Litterman assets allocation model and risk parity model, thereafter constructs a GAN, whose generator and discriminator are different LSTM networks. This paper chooses data from the constituent stocks of the CSI 500 Index between 2014 and 2019. Empirical results show that under this framework, the trading strategy gains 131% of annual absolute return in the 4-year back-test and only -27.8% max drawdown ratio. The framework successfully enhances the CSI500 Index from both high absolute return and low risk.

Readability, Opaqueness and Crash Risk
Undergrad Thesis (Best Paper), 2019

This paper empirically tests the relationship between the readability of the 10-K annual report, the transparency of financial statements and the crash risk using data from US firms that published 10-K between 1994 and 2013. The research shows that: (1) annual reports of firms with lower transparency is harder to read the MD&A Section; (2) firms with lower readability in 10-K are more prone to stock price crashes. By using Fog index from the computational linguistics, this paper links readability with earnings management, and helps further understand the impact of readability on stock price crash. It is of great significance for investors to identify crash risks and prevent stock prices from collapsing.

Evolution of Marketing Thoughts: A Graph Neural Network Approach
Hengxu Lin, working with Kamel Jedidi

This research studies how marketing thoughts emerge and spread between academia and industry over time. We applied embedding topic modeling on academic and practitioner articles and plan to use a graph neural network to build a genealogical graph that temporally links academic and practitioners’ articles.
